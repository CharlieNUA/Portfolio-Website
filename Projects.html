Projects

University Work

Over the two years of university i have had the chance to work on lots of different games that have improved my games designing techniques and coding knowledge massively. Some of these projects are not finished however they have been really fun to work on. As well as meeting new people in the industry along the way and strengthing my experience in games development.


Laser Beam

Link:Download

Link:Documentation

What I did: Originaly the design was a water management game that used a block to reflect water into a goal, however after working with Unreals Niagara system I thought this would take too long for the time we had set for our project. So instead this was turned into a laser beam game using Unreals line trace and calculating the reflection on mirror objects.Which i worked on as a solo project to get fully finished and playable with 20 levels.

Engine & Tools: Unreal engine 4, Rider 2019, Github, Photoshop

Space Cat Nip

Link:Download

Link:Documentation

What I did: Made for the Global Games Jam 2022, I worked on this with another Developer and a Artist to create a third-person shooter classic sci-fi over a couple of days. My main focus on this game was the robot enemies that chase the player around the map and also Spent lot of time on the gravity effect to make all the items and islands float which then would react to the player's movement.

Engine & Tools: Unreal engine 4, Visual Studio 2017, Github

Small Stalks

Link:Documentation

What I did: This game was my made as a big group project between two of every division, my job alongside an indie developer was to create a text-based game in ue4. However, our expectations of what we could get done were a lot more than we could so the game was left unfinished but enough for the project since it was only a game prototype. My main focus on this project was the entire communication between the player and the AI which lets the player talk to different characters and the choices/responses affect the environment around it.

Engine & Tools: Unreal engine 4, Rider 2019, Github, Mixamo
